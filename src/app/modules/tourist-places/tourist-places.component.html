Amruthadhara Falls

Amruthadhara Falls is a seasonal waterfall located in the East Godavari district of Andhra Pradesh, India. It is a popular destination for nature lovers, especially during the monsoon season when the falls are at their most spectacular. The falls are formed by the confluence of two streams, the Varaha and the Penna. They cascade down a series of rocky cliffs, creating a mesmerizing sight. The falls are surrounded by lush greenery, making for a truly picturesque setting.

Rampa Falls

In the semi-evergreen forests of the Agency track in East Godavari District, numerous picturesque spots and perennial waterfalls grace the landscape. Among them is Rampa V.S.S, surrounded by high forests showcasing Neelakanteswara and Rampa waterfalls. Sri Neelakanteswara Vana Vihara Sthali is located approximately 4 km from the village of Rampachodavaram in East Godavari District.

Maredumilli Falls

Maredumilli is a town in the East Godavari district of Andhra Pradesh, India. It is known for its waterfalls, which are a popular tourist destination. The most famous waterfalls in Maredumilli are the Rajatarangini Falls, the Amruthadhara Falls, and the Rampa Falls
